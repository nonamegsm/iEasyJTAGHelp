Overview

EasyJtagPlus hardware and software complex

The EasyJtagPlus hardware and software complex is intended for the automated restoration of firmware of mobile phones, smartphones, tablets and other electronic devices, hereinafter referred to as devices using the JTAG interface and/or eMMC memory chips.
 
The hardware part of the complex is a device, hereinafter referred to as box (Fig.1)

 

 

Fig. 1 - EasyJtagPlus Box Appearance

 

On the front of the box (Fig. 2) are four LEDs Blue, Red, Yellow and Green and connector JTAG (Header).

 

 

Fig. 2 – EasyJtagPlus box front panel

 

Glowing or flashing of LEDs indicate the status of the box.

The main JTAG connector is a 20-pin, two-line folder. The contacts of this connector are connected to the Jtag interface of the target device or its eMMC memory chip.

The RJ45 connector is auxiliary and is designed to enhance functionality of the box (USB-UART emulation, managed power lines, etc.). The default configuration of the RJ45 connector is shown in fig.3.


Fig. 3 - The default configuration of the RJ45 connector in the EasyJtagPlus box

On the back of the box (Fig. 4) there are RJ45 connectors, mini-USB and a slot for activation SIM card.

 

 
Fig. 4 - EasyJtagPlus box back panel

 

 

The mini-USB connector provides power to the box and its connection to the computer running the Z3X EasyJtagBox JTAG Classic Suite software.

 

A SIM card is inserted into the SIM slot, which is the hardware key to activate the box. Without the hardware key, EasyJtagBox is impossible to operate. 

The software part of the hardware and software complex includes the Z3X EasyJtagBox JTAG Classic Suite software and the USB port driver.
